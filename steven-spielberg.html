The Master of Wonder

After nearly 50 years, the director who defined modern blockbuster cinema returns to his first love: humanity's search for contact with the unknown.

Why Spielberg Returned to UFOs

Steven Spielberg has been fascinated with extraterrestrial life since childhood. His first major films—Close Encounters of the Third Kind (1977) and E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982)—established him as cinema's preeminent voice on alien contact. So why did he wait nearly 50 years to make another UFO film?

The answer lies in the changing conversation around UAPs (Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena). Congressional hearings, military footage releases, and whistleblower testimonies have shifted UFOs from fringe conspiracy to mainstream discourse. Spielberg has reportedly said the time finally felt "right" to tell this story.

From Close Encounters to Disclosure Day: Close Encounters showed humanity reaching out to make contact. Disclosure Day explores what happens when that contact has already occurred—and been hidden from the world.

Spielberg's UFO Filmography

Disclosure Day joins an extraordinary legacy of alien contact films:

  • Close Encounters of the Third Kind (1977) - A working-class electrician is drawn to a mysterious mountain where humans and aliens make first contact. Oscar-nominated.
  • E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982) - A boy befriends a stranded alien. The highest-grossing film of its era and a cultural phenomenon.
  • War of the Worlds (2005) - Hostile alien invasion threatens humanity. A darker take on contact.
  • Disclosure Day (2026) - The truth about extraterrestrial contact is revealed to the world.

What makes Spielberg unique is his ability to ground extraordinary events in human emotion. His aliens aren't just special effects—they're catalysts for deeply personal stories.

The Spielberg Touch

Spielberg's directorial style has several hallmarks that make his alien films unique:

  • Visual wonder - Imagery that captures awe and terror simultaneously
  • Suburban settings - Extraordinary events happening to ordinary people
  • Father/child dynamics - Family relationships at the emotional core
  • Practical effects preference - CGI enhanced but not dominated
  • John Williams scores - Iconic music that elevates every scene
  • Janusz Kaminski cinematography - Their partnership spans 30 years

Based on early reports, Disclosure Day continues these traditions while incorporating new themes relevant to the modern UAP conversation.

His Team on Disclosure Day

Spielberg has assembled his trusted collaborators:

  • David Koepp (Screenwriter) - Their fifth collaboration, following Jurassic Park and War of the Worlds
  • John Williams (Composer) - The legendary composer's 30th film with Spielberg
  • Janusz Kaminski (Cinematography) - Oscar-winning DP, working with Spielberg since Schindler's List
  • Kristie Macosko Krieger (Producer) - Long-time Amblin producer
