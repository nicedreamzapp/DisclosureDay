Who Wrote Disclosure Day?

David Koepp wrote the screenplay for Disclosure Day, marking his fifth collaboration with director Steven Spielberg. Koepp is one of the most commercially successful screenwriters in Hollywood history, with his films grossing over $6 billion worldwide.

The partnership between Koepp and Spielberg has produced some of cinema's most iconic moments, from the T-Rex attack in Jurassic Park to the alien invasion in War of the Worlds. With Disclosure Day, they return to science fiction territory with a story about humanity's first contact with extraterrestrial life.

Why Koepp for Disclosure Day? Koepp has a proven ability to balance spectacle with character-driven storytelling. His scripts are known for building tension methodically while maintaining emotional cores that resonate with audiences.

David Koepp + Spielberg: A History

The Koepp-Spielberg collaboration is one of the most successful writer-director partnerships in cinema:

  • Jurassic Park (1993) - Adapted Michael Crichton's novel into the groundbreaking blockbuster
  • The Lost World: Jurassic Park (1997) - Continued the dinosaur saga
  • War of the Worlds (2005) - Reimagined the classic alien invasion story
  • Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull (2008) - Brought Indy back
  • Disclosure Day (2026) - Their most ambitious collaboration yet

What makes this partnership work is their shared understanding of how to make extraordinary events feel personal. In Jurassic Park, it's not just about dinosaurs—it's about a man learning to connect with children. In War of the Worlds, the alien invasion serves as backdrop to a father trying to protect his family.

Disclosure Day appears to follow this template: a global event (alien contact) filtered through intimate human stories (Emily Blunt's meteorologist, Josh O'Connor's whistleblower).

David Koepp's Writing Style

Koepp's screenplays share several distinctive qualities that have made him Hollywood's go-to writer for intelligent blockbusters:

  • Slow-burn tension - Building dread through anticipation rather than constant action
  • Grounded characters - Protagonists feel like real people, not action heroes
  • Scientific plausibility - Research-based details that add credibility
  • Moral complexity - Villains have understandable motivations
  • Efficient exposition - Conveying information without obvious info-dumps

For Disclosure Day, these skills are crucial. The film reportedly balances multiple perspectives on extraterrestrial contact—scientific, military, political, and personal—requiring careful narrative juggling.

What We Know About the Script

While plot details remain closely guarded, several elements of Koepp's screenplay have emerged:

  • Multiple perspectives - The story follows various characters experiencing disclosure differently
  • Emotional depth - Colman Domingo revealed he "cried multiple times" reading the script
  • Mystery elements - The "seven billion" line suggests hidden revelations
  • Government intrigue - Josh O'Connor's whistleblower storyline
  • First contact focus - Communication over conflict

The emotional reactions from cast members suggest Koepp has written something that transcends typical blockbuster fare. When a respected actor like Colman Domingo says a script made him cry, it indicates substantial character work beyond spectacle.
