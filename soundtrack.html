The answer fans hoped for: John Williams IS composing the score for Disclosure Day. Announced in October 2025, this marks his historic 30th collaboration with Steven Spielberg.

Spielberg-Williams Alien Scores

Williams has composed the music for all of Spielberg's major alien-themed films:

  • Close Encounters of the Third Kind (1977) Oscar Nominated
  • E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982) Oscar Winner
  • War of the Worlds (2005) Grammy Nominated

Each score masterfully captured the emotional essence of first contact - wonder in Close Encounters and E.T., terror in War of the Worlds. A Williams score for Disclosure Day would bring this full circle.

What to Expect Musically

Based on Spielberg's alien filmography and modern scoring trends, Disclosure Day's score will likely feature:

Emotional Themes

A central theme for humanity's reaction to contact - something that captures both wonder and uncertainty. Expect a memorable main motif that evolves throughout the film.

Otherworldly Elements

Alien contact scores often incorporate unusual instrumentation, electronic elements, or unconventional sonic textures to represent the unknown. Close Encounters famously used its five-note communication motif.

Human Drama

Character-driven emotional cues for Emily Blunt's arc and the human relationships at the story's core. Spielberg films always balance spectacle with intimate character moments.

Scale and Grandeur

Full orchestral compositions for the film's bigger moments - the revelation, first contact, climactic sequences. Spielberg's films demand scores of epic scope.
