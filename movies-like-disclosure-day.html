15 Movies Like Disclosure Day

The ultimate alien contact watchlist before June 12, 2026

Can't wait for Disclosure Day? These films explore similar themes of first contact, government secrecy, and humanity's response to extraterrestrial life. Watch them all before Spielberg's new epic arrives.

Essential Viewing

#1

Close Encounters of the Third Kind

1977 Dir: Steven Spielberg MUST WATCH SPIELBERG

The definitive first contact film and Spielberg's original UFO masterpiece. A working-class man becomes obsessed with a vision after a close encounter, leading him to Devil's Tower where humanity and aliens finally meet.

Disclosure Day Connection: This is essential viewing. Disclosure Day is Spielberg's return to UFO cinema after nearly 50 years. Understanding Close Encounters helps contextualize everything about the new film.
Prime Video Apple TV
#2

Arrival

2016 Dir: Denis Villeneuve MUST WATCH

A linguist is recruited to communicate with mysterious alien visitors. Unlike most alien films, Arrival focuses on language, time, and the emotional weight of contact. Amy Adams delivers a career-defining performance.

Disclosure Day Connection: Like Disclosure Day, Arrival focuses on communication over conflict. Both films feature female protagonists who become humanity's bridge to the unknown.
Paramount+ Hulu
#3

E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial

1982 Dir: Steven Spielberg SPIELBERG

A boy befriends a stranded alien in this emotional masterpiece that became the highest-grossing film of its era. Spielberg's most personal alien film explores friendship across species.

Disclosure Day Connection: While E.T. is intimate where Disclosure Day is global, both share Spielberg's belief that contact could be wondrous rather than terrifying.
Peacock
#4

Contact

1997 Dir: Robert Zemeckis

Jodie Foster plays a SETI scientist who receives humanity's first message from an extraterrestrial civilization. Based on Carl Sagan's novel, it explores the intersection of science, faith, and bureaucracy.

Disclosure Day Connection: Both films feature female scientists at the center of first contact. Contact's portrayal of government response to alien signals may preview Disclosure Day's approach.
Max
#5

Signs

2002 Dir: M. Night Shyamalan

A former priest discovers crop circles on his farm as alien invasion begins worldwide. Shyamalan's masterful tension-building creates one of the most suspenseful alien films ever made.

Disclosure Day Connection: Signs shows ordinary people caught in extraordinary events - likely similar to Emily Blunt's meteorologist in Disclosure Day.
Prime Video

Government & Conspiracy

#6

The X-Files: Fight the Future

1998 Dir: Rob Bowman

Mulder and Scully uncover a government conspiracy involving alien colonization. The definitive government-UFO-conspiracy film that defined a generation's view of disclosure.

Disclosure Day Connection: Josh O'Connor's whistleblower character echoes X-Files themes of insiders fighting to reveal hidden truths.
#7

War of the Worlds

2005 Dir: Steven Spielberg SPIELBERG

Spielberg's darker take on alien contact - hostile invasion seen through a father trying to protect his children. Visceral and terrifying, showing the director's range with extraterrestrial themes.

Disclosure Day Connection: Demonstrates Spielberg's ability to portray various types of alien contact - from wonder (E.T.) to terror (War of the Worlds). Disclosure Day may blend both.
Paramount+
#8

Independence Day

1996 Dir: Roland Emmerich

Blockbuster alien invasion that defined 90s cinema. While more action-focused than Disclosure Day likely will be, it captures the scale of global response to extraterrestrial contact.

Disclosure Day Connection: Both films deal with worldwide disclosure and government response, though Disclosure Day appears to emphasize mystery over spectacle.
Disney+

Thought-Provoking Sci-Fi

#9

Interstellar

2014 Dir: Christopher Nolan

While not strictly first contact, Interstellar explores humanity's place in the cosmos and mysterious higher-dimensional beings. Emotional and scientifically ambitious.

Disclosure Day Connection: Both films ask big questions about humanity's relationship with unknown intelligence.
Paramount+
#10

District 9

2009 Dir: Neill Blomkamp

Aliens arrive not as conquerors but as refugees, confined to South African slums. A powerful allegory about how humanity might actually treat extraterrestrial visitors.

Disclosure Day Connection: Explores what happens after disclosure - how society adapts to the presence of aliens. The "seven billion" theory suggests similar themes.
Netflix
#11

The Day the Earth Stood Still

1951 Dir: Robert Wise

The classic that defined alien visitor films. An extraterrestrial arrives with a warning for humanity. Thoughtful and timeless.

Disclosure Day Connection: Both films present aliens with intent beyond invasion - visitors with something to communicate.
#12

Annihilation

2018 Dir: Alex Garland

A biologist leads an expedition into an expanding alien zone. Cerebral and haunting, exploring contact as transformation rather than communication.

Disclosure Day Connection: Emily Blunt's character transformation in the trailers echoes Annihilation's themes of alien influence changing humans.
Paramount+

Hidden Gems

#13

Fire in the Sky

1993 Dir: Robert Lieberman

Based on Travis Walton's alleged real abduction. A logger disappears for five days after a UFO encounter. The abduction sequence remains one of cinema's most disturbing.

Disclosure Day Connection: If Disclosure Day explores the darker side of contact, this film provides context for abduction narratives in UFO lore.
#14

Nope

2022 Dir: Jordan Peele

Horse trainers discover something terrifying in the sky above their California ranch. Peele's take on the UFO genre combines spectacle with commentary on exploitation.

Disclosure Day Connection: Both films approach UFOs from unexpected angles - not typical alien invasion fare.
Peacock
#15

Midnight Special

2016 Dir: Jeff Nichols

A father goes on the run to protect his son who possesses special powers that may connect to something beyond Earth. Quiet, emotional, Spielberg-influenced.

Disclosure Day Connection: Like early Spielberg, Nichols grounds the extraordinary in intimate family dynamics - an approach Disclosure Day seems to share.
