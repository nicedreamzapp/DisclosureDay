Who Does Eve Hewson Play?

Eve Hewson plays Dr. Rachel Torres, a scientist working within the government's response to extraterrestrial contact. Her character appears to be part of the scientific team trying to understand what the visitors want and how to communicate with them.

Dr. Torres likely works alongside other scientists and officials, including Wyatt Russell's Dr. James Chen and Colin Firth's Director Harold Kane. Her role may involve analyzing data, studying alien technology, or serving as a bridge between scientific and political responses.

Character Theory: Given Hewson's talent for playing characters with moral complexity (as demonstrated in Bad Sisters), Dr. Torres may face difficult ethical choices about what information to share or withhold from the public.

Previous Spielberg Connection

Interestingly, Eve Hewson has already worked with Steven Spielberg—she appeared in Bridge of Spies (2015), playing Carol Donovan, the daughter of Tom Hanks' character James Donovan.

This prior collaboration may have influenced her casting in Disclosure Day. Spielberg has a history of returning to actors he's worked with before, suggesting he sees significant potential in Hewson's talent.

About Eve Hewson

Born in Dublin, Ireland, Eve Hewson is the daughter of U2 frontman Bono and activist Ali Hewson. Despite her famous parentage, she has carved out an impressive career on her own merits, studying acting at NYU's Tisch School of the Arts.

Her breakthrough role came in Steven Soderbergh's Cinemax series The Knick, where she played Nurse Lucy Elkins opposite Clive Owen. More recently, her performance as Becka Garvey in Bad Sisters earned critical acclaim and wider recognition.
