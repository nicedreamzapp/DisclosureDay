Who Does Emily Blunt Play?

Emily Blunt stars as Dr. Sarah Chen, a meteorologist who becomes the unlikely first point of contact between humanity and extraterrestrial visitors. Her performance is central to the film's emotional core.

Dr. Chen is working at a weather monitoring station when she detects an anomalous signal that doesn't match any known natural or man-made pattern. Her investigation leads to something that will change not only her life but the fate of humanity.

The Role: Emily Blunt's character experiences what appears to be alien possession or communication. She begins speaking in clicking sounds and unknown languages, her eyes display strange patterns, and she becomes a bridge between two species.

Character Analysis

Dr. Sarah Chen represents the "ordinary person in extraordinary circumstances" archetype that Spielberg has explored throughout his career‚Äîfrom Roy Neary in Close Encounters to Elliott in E.T.

What makes Sarah unique is her scientific background. Unlike previous Spielberg protagonists who stumbled into contact, Sarah is trained to observe, measure, and analyze. When the unanalyzable happens to her, it challenges everything she knows.

The Possession Question

Is Sarah being possessed against her will, or has she been chosen as a willing translator? The trailer suggests ambiguity‚Äîmoments of terror alongside moments of transcendence. This duality likely forms the emotional journey of her character.
