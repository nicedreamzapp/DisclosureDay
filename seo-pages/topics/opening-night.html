✅ Opening Night Checklist

1
Book Tickets Early IMAX and premium formats sell out fast. Set a reminder for when tickets go on sale (typically 2-3 weeks before release).
2
Choose Your Format IMAX recommended for the full Spielberg experience. Dolby Cinema is excellent alternative.
3
Arrive Early For opening night, arrive 30-45 minutes early to get good seats and soak in the atmosphere.
4
Go Spoiler-Free Avoid social media after international premieres. Some markets may see it hours before you.
5
Stay for Credits Spielberg films sometimes have post-credit moments. Plus: John Williams deserves recognition.

Pro Tips

Book Center Seats: For IMAX, aim for center of the theater, about 2/3 back from the screen for optimal viewing.
Use the Bathroom: Runtime TBA, but Spielberg films often run 2+ hours. Go before it starts.
Phone Off: Not just silent — off. The screen glow ruins it for everyone.
Bring Tissues: It's Spielberg. It's John Williams. There will be emotions.
Avoid Thursday Socials: International markets may release hours earlier. Mute keywords.

The Shared Experience

There's something magical about seeing a Spielberg film on opening night. The collective gasps. The shared tension. The applause when John Williams' score swells. Disclosure Day is designed for this experience.

Don't wait for streaming. Don't let someone spoil it. Be there when the world discovers the truth together.
