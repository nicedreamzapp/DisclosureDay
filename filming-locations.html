Where Was Disclosure Day Filmed?

Complete guide to filming locations

Primary Filming Locations

New Mexico, USA Primary Location

The majority of Disclosure Day was filmed in New Mexico, continuing the state's long history as a UFO-related filming location. The desert landscapes provide both practical locations and thematic resonance—New Mexico is home to Roswell and has been associated with UFO sightings for decades.

Specific Areas:

  • Albuquerque - Studio work and urban scenes
  • Santa Fe - Government building exteriors
  • Remote desert locations - The climactic contact sequences
  • White Sands area - Military base exteriors
United Kingdom Studio & Locations

Studio work and some interior scenes were filmed in the UK, likely at facilities familiar to Spielberg from previous productions. The UK offers world-class studio facilities and skilled crews.

Specific Areas:

  • Shepperton Studios - Likely soundstage work
  • Pinewood Studios - Major interior sets
  • London - Possible exterior shots
Washington D.C. Area Government Scenes

Scenes involving government buildings and the political response to disclosure were filmed in and around Washington D.C. These sequences feature recognizable landmarks that ground the story in political reality.

Specific Areas:

  • Pentagon area - Exterior establishing shots
  • D.C. government buildings - Briefing room scenes
  • Virginia - Intelligence agency exteriors

Why New Mexico?

New Mexico has become synonymous with UFO lore in American culture. The choice to film there is both practical and symbolic:

  • Roswell connection - The state's UFO history adds authenticity
  • Diverse landscapes - Desert, mountains, and small-town America all available
  • Film incentives - New Mexico offers competitive production tax credits
  • Spielberg history - Close Encounters also featured desert sequences
  • Practical lighting - Clear skies ideal for night filming
Thematic Choice: By filming in New Mexico, Spielberg grounds Disclosure Day in the real-world geography of American UFO culture. The state has been central to UFO narratives since 1947—this isn't just a location choice, it's a statement about the film's connection to actual UAP history.

Set Design

Production designer reports suggest several major sets were constructed:

  • Weather monitoring station - Sarah Chen's workplace where she receives the signal
  • Government command center - Where officials coordinate the response
  • Classified research facility - The "mind control" equipment location
  • Contact site - The location where disclosure occurs

Spielberg's preference for practical sets over pure CGI environments suggests these locations will feel grounded and tactile, enhancing the realism of extraordinary events.

Visual Style

Cinematographer Janusz Kaminski (Spielberg's long-time collaborator) has developed a distinctive visual approach for his alien films:

  • Practical lighting - Real light sources over artificial setups
  • Lens flares - Strategic use to suggest otherworldly presence
  • Low-light capability - Maintaining atmosphere in dark scenes
  • Widescreen composition - Emphasizing vast landscapes and sky
